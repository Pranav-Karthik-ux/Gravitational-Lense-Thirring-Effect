# Lense–Thirring Effect (Frame Dragging)

![Lense–Thirring Orbital Precession](images/precession.png)

## Overview
The **Lense–Thirring effect** is a prediction of General Relativity where the rotation of a massive body drags the surrounding spacetime. This phenomenon produces small deviations in the motion of nearby objects, resulting in **orbital precession**.

This project studies the **gravitomagnetic effects of rotating masses** using the weak-field approximation of Einstein’s field equations and highlights the analogy between **gravity and electromagnetism**. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## Weak Field Approximation
In weak gravitational fields, spacetime can be approximated as a small perturbation of flat Minkowski spacetime. This simplifies Einstein’s field equations and allows them to resemble Maxwell’s equations.

The metric is written as:
$$
g_{\mu \nu}=\eta_{\mu \nu}+ h_{\mu \nu}
$$
